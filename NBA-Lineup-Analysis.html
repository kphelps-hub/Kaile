NBA Lineup Analysis

This project seeks to provide tools for looking at how teams' lineups change between the NBA Playoffs and the Regular Season. I focused on whether teams preferred to play undersized players during the playoffs and how that trend has changed overtime. I considered undersized players to be players 2 centimeters shorter than the average player for that position on the respective team, for that year.

  • Tableau
  • Git-Hub

Project Steps

  1. All data for this project was pulled from pbpstats.com using a Python script.
  2. Output was compiled and merged in Python.
  3. Data was loaded into a database and cleaned and transformed in SQLite.
  4. Visualizations were created from resulting queries using Tableau.

Results

Undersized Percentage (Pct) Over Time

A visualization of how often teams played undersized players over time.

Undersized Playoff to Regular Season Ratio

A visualization broken down by position comparing the frequency of playing undersized players in the playoffs.

Undersized Scatter Plot

An interactive visualization to identify teams that played significantly different lineups in the regular season vs in the playoffs.

Team Lineup Distribution

A tool to look at how individual teams used players at different positions.
